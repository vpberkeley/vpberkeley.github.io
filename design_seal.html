This figure shows how the Top-Side Assembly functions. The left image of the figure represents the portion of the assembly that seals to the top of the egg. The teal colored part allows the assembly to have a greater sealing surface area with the egg. The linear motion of the top seal is implemented with a gear and pitch rack. The pitch rack is bonded to the protrusion on the cylindrical part. The rack is constrained to only vertical movement with the use of two rails that protrude from the motor mounting plate.




This figure shows the full assembly attached to the main body.




Mounting the stepper motors onto the cylindrical housing of the main body is done with the use of curved 3D printed spacers and mounting brackets, as shown in the above. The bracket has holes that line up with the threaded holes on the NEMA-17 and it is tightened to the main body using bolts and hex nuts that remain captive in the inside spacer.
